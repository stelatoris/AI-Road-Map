Reinforcement Learning

Markov Decision Processes

Markov decision processes are a mathematical framework for modeling decision-making problems. Topics to focus on include:

  • States and actions
  • Rewards and value functions
  • Policy and optimal policy
  • Discounted future rewards and Bellman equations

Q-Learning

Q-learning is a popular reinforcement learning algorithm that learns a policy by iteratively improving an estimate of the optimal action-value function. Topics to focus on include:

  • Off-policy learning and the Q-learning algorithm
  • Epsilon-greedy exploration strategy
  • Experience replay and target network
  • Deep Q-networks and double Q-learning

Policy Gradients

Policy gradients is a family of reinforcement learning algorithms that directly learn a parameterized policy that maximizes the expected reward. Topics to focus on include:

  • Policy gradients and the policy gradient theorem
  • REINFORCE algorithm and baseline
  • Actor-critic algorithm and advantage function
  • Proximal policy optimization (PPO) and trust region policy optimization (TRPO)

Actor-Critic Methods

Actor-critic methods are a family of reinforcement learning algorithms that learn both a policy and a value function. Topics to focus on include:

  • Actor-critic architecture and the advantage function
  • A3C and A2C algorithms
  • Distributed reinforcement learning

Model-Based Reinforcement Learning

Model-based reinforcement learning is a family of reinforcement learning algorithms that learn a model of the environment and use it to improve the policy. Topics to focus on include:

  • Model-based and model-free reinforcement learning
  • Model-based algorithms, such as Dyna and MBIE
  • Planning and Monte Carlo tree search (MCTS)
